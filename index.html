The lone, solitary scream of agony
Of the clouds ripped through the dark silence.
The bass of the noise- an unorganized symphony
For all that it craves and for which lost it’s sense.

The misery of the clouds as water, its lifeline
Threatens to leave was one no language can express
As the sky glooms over, not even the sun’s mighty shine
Could brighten up the clouds that were depressed

The clouds’ screams that we call thunder,
Begs the water to not leave it alone, to stay true to their promise
But the damage had been done; the cloud had committed the blunder
Gone were their beautiful days of happiness and bliss. The clouds can’t express their emotions in words
So they pour out their sadness and anger in the form of thunder
The water takes no heed to the screams, the curses
‘How could you be so cruel’ the by passers wonder.

Oceans to fill and fields to water, the rain holds its head up proud
It has to go down in the form of pellets of rain
But in this process inhibits the very existence of clouds
And no matter what the cloud had done, it didn’t deserve the pain.

‘I have duties to fulfill and you have wronged me’ the water smiles
‘One more chance’ the clouds beg the water that’s slowly slipping away
The rain maintained the deadly silence for a while
‘For in the end, who are you without me?’ with those parting words, the water slipped away.
